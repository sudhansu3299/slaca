## Design Context

### Users
Collections specialists and compliance leads at mid-market lenders. They launch new borrower outreaches, monitor active conversations, and review transcript quality to decide whether to escalate, settle, or re-run simulations.

### Brand Personality
Calm, clinical, unwavering. The interface should project measured authority: no theatrics, no fluff, but an unmistakable sense of control and preparedness.

### Aesthetic Direction
Minimalist light foundation with disciplined indigo and graphite accents inspired by bank counter forms. Layouts should feel like archival paperwork placed on a spotless desk: generous margins, asymmetric grids, subtle use of delineation instead of ornament. Avoid neon gradients, glassmorphism, or anything playful. Reference points: Stripe’s internal risk consoles, Notion’s calm document surfaces, Revolut’s compliance tooling (for typography cadence). Anti-reference: marketing-heavy landing pages or SaaS dashboards drenched in purple/blue gradients.

### Design Principles
1. **Measured clarity** – keep typography, spacing, and iconography restrained so loan data reads instantly.
2. **Decisive affordances** – CTAs and pills must look actionable without glow or gimmicks; rely on weight, not color noise.
3. **Transparent audit trail** – expose status, timestamps, and personas in structured blocks so compliance review needs zero digging.
4. **Calm resilience** – light theme with tinted neutrals that feel stable; use darker overlays sparingly for focus without aggression.
